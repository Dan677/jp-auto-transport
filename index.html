Seasonal Car Shipping: Moving Your Vehicle Safely in Winter and Summer

Shipping a car during extreme weather seasons requires extra planning and care. Winter brings snow, ice, and road salt, all of which can pose risks to your vehicle during transit. In contrast, summer shipping often involves high temperatures and heavy traffic, which can affect delivery times. At JP Auto Transport, we adapt to seasonal challenges by using experienced drivers and tailored transport solutions. Enclosed trailers provide protection from harsh conditions, while open carriers remain a cost-effective choice for most routes. Regardless of the season, preparation is key. Checking antifreeze levels, securing your battery, and documenting your car‚Äôs condition are important steps to ensure a smooth transport experience.

Top Tips for Preparing Your Car for Shipping

Before shipping your car, a few simple steps can make the entire process smoother and safer. Start by cleaning your vehicle inside and out so you can easily document its condition with photos. Remove all personal items, as they are not covered by transport insurance and can shift during transit. Check tire pressure, battery charge, and fluid levels to avoid leaks or mechanical issues on the trailer. Fold in mirrors and remove any custom accessories that could break off. These preparations not only save time but also reduce the risk of unexpected problems. A well-prepared car helps ensure a stress-free delivery and a better overall transport experience.

Size and weight of vehicle

Larger or heavier vehicles need special accommodations, which can increase transport costs.

The car condition
